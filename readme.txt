*********************
Software Requirements
*********************
To operate the Introduction to Numerical Methods CD ROM, Microsoft
Windows 95, 98,2000, ME, or NT 4.x, must be installed on the host computer.
In addition, the user will also need Matlab 5 or higher installed.   

*********************
Hardware Requirements
*********************
The recommended hardware requirements to operate the Introduction to Numerical Methods are listed below.
 Pentium 1 processor or higher
 16 MB RAM.
 Hard drive with 15 MB of available disk space. 
 CD-ROM drive (4x).
 SVGA monitor at 800 x 600, 256 colors.

***************
CD ROM Contents
***************
The CD-ROM contains the following MATLAB m-functions:

abash.m : Adams-Bashford of orders 2,3, and 4  section  9.6  
amoulton.m : Adams-Moulton method section  9.8 
bisect.m: Bisection method section 3.1 
derive.m Differentiation section   8.1 
euler.m: Euler's method section 9.1  
explsqr.m: Least squares fit, exponential section 7.3 
false.m: False position method section 3.2  
finitediff.m: Finite difference methods section 10.1 
fixed.m: Fixed point iteration section 3.6  
gausselm: Gaussian elimination, scaled pivoting section 4.3  
gauss_quad.m: Gaussian quadrature section 8.4 
heat.m: Parabolic differential equations section 12.1	 
heat_crank.m: Parabolic differential equations section 12.1	
hyperboli.cm: Hyperbolic differential equations section 12.2 
hyplsqr.m: Least squares fit, hyperbolic section 7.3 
jacobi.m: Jacobi iteration section 4.6 
lagrange.m: Lagrange interpolation polynomial section 5.4 
laplace.m: Laplace's equation section 12.3 
linlsqr.m: Least squares fit, linear section 7.1  
lshoot.m: Linear shooting method section 10.2 
lufact.m: LU decomposition section 4.4 
midpoint.m: Midpoint method section 9.4  
newton.m: Newton's method section 3.4   
newton\_sys.m: Newton's method for systems section 3.8   
newton2.m: Newton's method, multiple roots section 3.7 
newtondd.m: Interpolation, Newton differences section 5.2  
ngaussel.m: Gaussian elimination, naive  section 4.2 
polylsqr.m: Least squares fit, polynomial section 7.2 
rk2\_4.m: Runge-Kutta method, orders 2 and 4 section 9.4 
romberg.m: Romberg integration section 8.3  
secant.m: Secant method  section 3.5 
seidel.m: Gauss-Seidel iterationsection 4.7 
simpson.m: Simpson's rule  section 8.2  
spl1.m: Spline, linear section 6.1  
spl3.m: Spline, cubic section 6.3  
sys\_rk4.m: System of differential equations section 9.11  
trapez.m: Trapezoidal rule section 8.1
